Your Robocall Report: Not a Void, But a Vital Evidence File

When you report a robocall with RoboCallMeNot, your information is directed to specific agencies. These reports are crucial data points for ongoing investigations and enforcement efforts.

Read the file Open the app
Exhibit A

Agency Directives and Enforcement

The FTC utilizes Do Not Call complaints to build cases against repeat violators, with the authority to fine companies ignoring the registry. Concurrently, the FCC directly regulates telecom carriers, enabling them to require blocking traffic from providers originating high volumes of illegal robocalls. The FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) aggregates reports to identify patterns and organized fraud operations across many victims.

The Cumulative Impact of Reporting

While a single report rarely stops a scammer immediately, each submission becomes an integral part of a larger evidence pattern. Regulators depend on this aggregated data to inform their actions. This systematic collection helps identify broader trends and targets for intervention.

Exhibit C

Your Action Contributes to Resolution

Understanding that each report contributes to a larger investigative effort underscores the importance of consistent reporting. Your participation provides valuable data points that aid in the identification and disruption of illicit activities. By reporting, you help strengthen the collective defense against unwanted and illegal calls.
