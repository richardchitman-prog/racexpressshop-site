Recurring Robocall Scams: Understanding the Patterns

Certain scam scripts reappear consistently. Understanding these common tactics can assist in identification, and RoboCallMeNot provides a tool for logging and reporting such calls.

Exhibit A

Analysis of Persistent Robocall Categories

Auto warranty robocalls have remained one of the most consistently reported categories to the FTC for several years running. Tech support scams, where a caller claims your computer is compromised, led to $159 million in reported losses among older consumers in 2024 alone. Government imposter scams rely on manufactured urgency and threats of immediate consequences.

Exhibit B

Recognizing Common Scam Modus Operandi

Government imposter scams, featuring callers claiming to be Social Security, the IRS, or law enforcement, often employ manufactured urgency and threats, tactics real agencies do not use over the phone. Similarly, package delivery and 'your order could not be shipped' scam calls and texts reliably spike around major shopping holidays, exploiting seasonal activity.
