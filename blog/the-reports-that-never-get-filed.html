Robocalls: The Unreported Challenge to Enforcement

The scale of unwanted robocalls far outpaces the rate of official reporting, creating a significant barrier to enforcement. RoboCallMeNot streamlines the process, making it easier to file official reports.

Read the file Open the app
Exhibit A

The Reporting Discrepancy

In 2025, only approximately 1 in 32,571 robocalls received were reported to the FTC, despite 1,597,063 robocall complaints reaching the agency. This occurs even though 92% of Americans have experienced an unwanted spam call.

1 in 32,571 robocalls received in 2025 actually got reported to the FTC
Exhibit B

Impact on Official Registries

The low rate of reporting means that enforcement agencies lack the full scope of the problem. For example, two out of three Do Not Call Registry complaints filed in 2025 specifically concerned a robocall, highlighting their pervasive nature.

Exhibit C

Taking Action Against Robocalls

Accurate and widespread reporting is essential for authorities to effectively address the issue of unwanted calls. By simplifying the reporting process, individuals can contribute valuable data to enforcement efforts. This collective action strengthens the ability of agencies to combat robocall operations.
